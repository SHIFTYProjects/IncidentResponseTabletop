⚠️ BOTH GAMES ARE MEANT TO BE PROCTORED BY SOMEONE TO LEAD THE DISCUSSION

Game 1 - IT Focused Workshop

You have a Modest budget of $500,000 to choose your software purchases for the year and protect your organization.

Phase 1: Protection

Choose your Protection Products carefully as a group, as they will be the only thing to protect you from the incidents that are about to occur (Injects).

  • You must select at least 1 from each category for the game to begin.
  • Keep spending until you can't make any more purchases.

Phase 2: Execution

Once chosen click "Start Game". This will advance you to the next page and allow you to draw an Inject Card.

You will then be given a scenario that you will either be:

  • Protected: (Matching Software) +5 Points
  • Not Protected: (No Software) -15 Points
Group Discussion Questions:
  • Were you protected? Would you have been in your actual environment from this type of incident?
  • How did you remediate? How would you remediate your environment?
  • What other departments would this have affected other than just IT? Who else would need to be notified?

After discussion, click the "VIEW REAL WORLD IMPACT & REMEDIATION" button.

GOALS: Create awareness of different outages. Use Fillable workshop sheets to develop or fully realize backup plans. A good first question to ask is: Where did this information come from? Then follow the chain of command up the food chain until resolution. Can you survive 5 rounds?

Game 2 - Emergency Response

Setup

Choose your Departments that will be participating in the exercise. Select your number of rounds, and a timer if you want to add a sense of urgency.

The Exercise

Read the inject. After each inject is drawn, fill out the fillable response worksheet either as departments or as a team and discuss your findings with the group.

Rating & Analysis

  • Rate your response: Use the slider to report whether the department had No plan, a current plan, or somewhere in between.
  • Impact Mapping: Select the other departments that this would affect.
  • Progress: Click "Submit & Next" to move to the next question.
Key Insight: It's not always easy to tell who or what will be affected, but all departments are tied together in many ways. A good first question to ask is: Where did this information come from? Then follow the chain of command up the food chain until resolution.
GOALS: Create awareness of different types of outages. Use Fillable workshop sheets as a starting point to develop/realize backup plans.
