# IncidentResponseTabletop
Incident Response Tabletop Games.  Cybersecurity and Disaster Focused.  Designed to be ran as an exercise with your staff for better awareness and to assist in planning for the inevitable emergencies we all encounter.


There are 2 Small Portable Games that are meant to be Facilitated by 1-2 indiduals to lead a discussion after each incident.



**GAME 1**
IT Focused IR Workshop

CybersecurityWorkshopGame
Portable HTML Tabletop Exercise Game for Admins and IT pros alike.

Loosly based on recovery NIST standards and real world tactics.

Tabletop Group Excersise

• Put all your teams into groups by department

ie; Tranportation, Finance, HR, IT, etc.



• Select your protection cards as a whole company until you are out of money, making the best decisions you can with your budget. Your game starts with 50 Points to your company.

• Draw inject Cards - (Random assortment of around 40 different types of incidents.) Once a card is drawn you will take damage from up to 2 sources -15 points each for a total loss of -30 each round if you were not protected by the card.

• If you had a protection card you get +5 Points.

• After each inject card is drawn you fill out an incident response form saying if it affected your department and how you would remediate if it did/didnt affect you or would have. Each team/department then presents their results of the incident and Discusses the outcomes/remediation for that incident 1 round at a time with your team.

• Gameplay continues for 5 rounds or until you are out of points.

At the end of your gameplay The presentor has a HOTWASH - or summary of the events and how the gameplay afected each department and how it got them thinking about not just Cybersecurity incidents, but recovey plans in general and how each department has its piece of the puzzle.



**GAME 2**
Emergency and Administrator Focused Workshop

EmergencyResponseWorkshop
Emergency Response HTML workshop Game

• Choose your Active Departments (Categories you will like to have questions in.) 
• Choose Number of Rounds
• If you want to add a sense of urgency or have a strict time limit you can select a timer for each situation (Inject).
• Click start excersise

After each inject, select the affected departments and rate how well the primary department would handle the situation.  Do they have a good working plan?  10 points.  Do they have no plan? Awart 0 Points.  

Then fill out an incident response paper.  Either as a group, or if you have multiple departments, have each department fill one out, and report their findings to the group.
